Using Telemedicine to Improve Diabetes Care
A pilot study led by Jennifer Raymond, MD, found that virtual appointments significantly improved clinic attendance for young adults with type 1 diabetes.
In the study, 74 percent of patients using telemedicine met the American Diabetes Association recommendation of quarterly clinic appointments—compared with none of the patients using in-person visits. The telemedicine patients also actively sought medical care and advice outside their appointments. See how the study worked.
Dr. Raymond is our Associate Division Chief, Clinical Diabetes Director and Vice Chair of CHLA’s Executive Telehealth Committee. She is also co-leading a telemedicine initiative in Armenia.
Young adults with type 1 diabetes were much more likely to keep virtual clinic appointments.
Tackling Obesity in Prader-Willi Syndrome
Our new Prader-Willi Syndrome (PWS) Clinic helps families address obesity early on with a personalized lifestyle model adapted from our EMPOWER Weight Management Clinic. CHLA has 50 active PWS patients, and many travel great distances to come here.
The clinic recently became a site for a clinical trial studying carbetocin as a weight-loss medication in PWS and collaborates closely with CHLA researcher Sebastien Bouret, PhD. The team is led by Alaina Vidmar, MD, and includes a dietitian and physical therapist.
Advancing Early Care for PCOS
CHLA’s Adolescent Hyperandrogenism/Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) Clinic is one of the few in the nation devoted to the care of adolescent girls with PCOS.
The twice-monthly clinic, directed by David Geller, MD, PhD, provides early, specialized treatment for PCOS—aiming to prevent the serious consequences affecting adult women with the disorder. We are also conducting a natural history study of adolescent PCOS, which will enable us to participate in multicenter studies and novel therapeutic approaches.
Understanding the New CAH Guidelines
In March, I will be moderating a panel discussion at ENDO 2019 around the new congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH) guidelines, which were recently released by the Endocrine Society.
CHLA cares for one of the nation's largest CAH patient populations and is one of only four U.S. programs designated as a medical and surgical center of excellence by CARES Foundation. The clinic is co-led by myself and Mimi Kim, MD—recipient of the 2018 CARES Foundation Physician Pioneer Award.
